Theory

  • Week 1

    In our very first week we were introduced to the Principlas of Visual Design and HTML.

    Principals-
    Scale: Uing relative size to signal importance.
    Visual Heirarchy: Guiding the eye on the page so that it attends to different design elements.
    Balance: Satisfying arrangement or proportion of design elements.
    Contrast: Juxtaposition of visually dissimilar elements in order to convey the fact that these elements are different.
    Gestalt Principal: How humans simplify and organize complex images that consist of many elements.
    HTML-
    Stands for Hyper Text Markup Language We got an intro to the different tags, proper structure and how to make lists.

  • Week 2

    User Interface:
    We were shown how the user interface works and how it enabes users the reacy with a system.
    We also learned about Norman’s 6 Principles of Usability:
    Visibility, Feedback, Affordance, Mapping, Constraints and Consistency

  • Week 3

    Colours:
    We were taught how colour theory effects a website and how use it correctly.
    Colours set visual cues or reinforce a journey for the user.
    They also highlight or draw the users attention to a point you're trying to make.

  • Week 4

    Accessability:
    There are three fundamental levels:
    Level A (Must be followed to provide basic accessibility)
    Level AA (Provides access to larger groups of people with disabilities)
    Level AAA (Highest level of access to largest group of users)
    Content should be understandable, clear and have simple language.
    Adding text with illustrations or videos can be helpful.
    The users must be able to understand the features easily and be able to navigate the website.

  • Week 5

    World Wide Web:
    LAN (Local Area Network)- Usually confined to a single building or group of buildings
    WAN (Wide Area Network)- Form of public or commercial communications network to connect computers.
    We also went trhough the basis of the internet and how it works.

  • Week 6

    Flex:
    Flex allows elements to automatically position themselves depending on screen size. A flexbox is used.
    These are used within the css section.

  • Week 7

    Graphics:
    fGIF – Graphics Interchange Format
    JPG – Joint Photographic Experts Group
    PNG – Portable Network Graphics
    Alternate text content to convey the meaning/intent of the image

  • Week 8

    Designing for Mobile Phones:
    This can be done by minimise cognitive load, design an accessible interface and make the navigation simple.
    Having a version of your website that can be easily used on a mobile phone is impotant.
